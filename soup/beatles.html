    A staged way of learning that tricky F Em Dm C (I’m a beginner, myself, so please feel free to point out if this is a bad idea for some reason)
    Start by just ignoring the Em and Dm, and just playing F C, until you get the rest of the tune sorted.
    Once you’ve got the hang of that, play it as F C F C.
    When you’ve got your fingers moving quickly enough, try changing it to F Cmaj7 Dm C. (Cmaj7 is just one finger on the 2nd fret of the bottom string, and if you try to use your index finger, here – even though you usually wouldn’t for Cmaj7 – then it’s a gateway drug to the E minor chord)
    When you’ve mastered that (I haven’t, yet) you should be ready to move on to F Em Dm C.
